Orbital Mechanics

Satellites are propagated with the SGP4 model from two-line element (TLE) sets. Each frame the propagator returns Earth-Centered Inertial coordinates, which are rotated into Earth-Centered Earth-Fixed space using Greenwich Mean Sidereal Time — so every marker sits above its true ground position, in real time.

Live Day/Night Terminator

The globe blends NASA Blue Marble imagery with night-side city lights in a custom shader. The terminator is computed from a real solar ephemeris every quarter second — the sunlit hemisphere you see is the one lit right now.

Scale & Accuracy

Satellite positions and telemetry are computed from real element sets in real time. For visibility, marker sizes and low-orbit altitudes are exaggerated on screen, and the deep-space replay compresses planetary distances — as most professional aerospace visualisations do. Nothing in the numeric readouts is scaled.
